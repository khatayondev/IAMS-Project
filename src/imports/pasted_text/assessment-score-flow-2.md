ASSESSMENT & SCORE FLOW
Industrial Attachment Management System
Proposed Grading Structure — Pending Review
SCOPE — SEMESTRIAL ATTACHMENTS ONLY
This grading structure applies exclusively to semester-long (semestrial) industrial attachments. Shorter attachment types are outside the scope of this document and use a separate or simplified grading approach.

This document presents the proposed assessment and score flow for the semestrial Industrial Attachment programme. It covers who assesses what, the rating scales used, how qualitative ratings convert to numeric scores, and how the final grade is compiled. All weightings and values marked ⚠ are OPEN FOR DECISION and require sign-off before implementation.

1.  Overview of the Grading Structure
This grading structure applies to semestrial industrial attachments only. The final grade of every student on a semestrial attachment is compiled by the Departmental Liaison Officer (DLO) from four assessment sources. Each source contributes a score out of 100. The DLO applies per-department weightings — configured in advance — to produce a single final percentage out of 100.

#	Assessment Source	Assessed By	Frequency	Output
1	Industrial Supervisor Assessment	Industrial Supervisor	Single combined score	Score / 100
2	Site Visitation	Departmental Supervisor	Once (during term)	Score / 100
3	Attachment Report	DLO	Once (post-attachment)	Score / 100
4	Presentation	DLO / Panel	Once (post-attachment)	Score / 100

Source 1 forms the Industrial Supervisor component (40%). Source 2 contributes the Departmental Supervisor component (30%). Sources 3 and/or 4 form the Report & Presentation component (30%) — departments choose whether this component is fulfilled by a Report only, a Presentation only, or both. Per-department custom weighting is described in Section 5.

2.  Industrial Supervisor Assessment  (40%)
2.1  Overview
The Industrial Supervisor contributes to the student's grade through two distinct activities:

#	Activity	Purpose	Scored?
1	Weekly Assessment Rubric	Qualitative progress record — completed every week of the term (auto-calculated from term dates)	No — collected as a record only, not scored
2	Final Assessment Form	Comprehensive end-of-term competency assessment across 20 criteria rated 1–5	Yes — this is the sole source of the Industrial Supervisor Score out of 100

2.2  Weekly Assessment Rubric  (Qualitative Record — Not Scored)
The Industrial Supervisor completes one weekly form per student for every week of the attachment. The number of weeks is determined automatically by the system from the term start and end dates set by the CLO. These forms are not scored — they serve as a qualitative progress record that the DLO and Academic Supervisor can review.

#	Criterion	Scale	Rating Options
1	Attendance	4-point	[ ] Poor   [ ] Satisfactory   [ ] Good   [ ] Very Good
2	Discipline	4-point	[ ] Poor   [ ] Satisfactory   [ ] Good   [ ] Very Good
3	Punctuality	4-point	[ ] Poor   [ ] Satisfactory   [ ] Good   [ ] Very Good
4	Ability to complete work on schedule	3-point	[ ] Weak   [ ] Average   [ ] Above Average
5	Ability to work under pressure	3-point	[ ] Weak   [ ] Average   [ ] Above Average
6	General Aptitude	3-point	[ ] Weak   [ ] Average   [ ] Above Average

2.3  Final Assessment Form  (Scored — 20 Criteria)
At the end of the attachment, the Industrial Supervisor completes the Final Assessment Form. This is the sole scored assessment from the Industrial Supervisor. All 20 criteria are rated on a numeric scale of 1 to 5:
1 = Weak  |  2 = Below Average  |  3 = Average  |  4 = Good  |  5 = Outstanding

Section A — Specific Skills  (4 criteria)
#	Criterion	Scale
1	Technical Skills	1 – 5
2	Analytical Skills	1 – 5
3	Creative Skills	1 – 5
4	Communication Skills	1 – 5

Section B — General Employability Skills  (8 criteria)
#	Criterion	Scale
5	Ability to complete work on schedule	1 – 5
6	Ability to follow instructions carefully	1 – 5
7	Ability to take initiative	1 – 5
8	Ability to work with little supervision	1 – 5
9	Ability to work with other staff	1 – 5
10	Adherence to organisation's rules and regulations	1 – 5
11	Adherence to safety and environmental rules	1 – 5
12	Resourcefulness	1 – 5

Section C — Attitude to Work  (5 criteria)
#	Criterion	Scale
13	Attendance at work	1 – 5
14	Punctuality	1 – 5
15	Desire to work	1 – 5
16	Willingness to accept new ideas and suggestions	1 – 5
17	Loyalty to the organisation	1 – 5

Section D — Human Relations  (3 criteria)
#	Criterion	Scale
18	Relationship with co-workers	1 – 5
19	Relationship with superiors	1 – 5
20	Emotional stability	1 – 5

2.4  Final Assessment Score Calculation
Total criteria: 20  (A: 4, B: 8, C: 5, D: 3).  Each rated 1 – 5.
Max raw score	20 × 5 = 100 points
Normalised score	(Sum of all ratings ÷ 100) × 100  →  Industrial Supervisor Score out of 100

⚠  Decision needed (D-1): Should sections A, B, C, D be equally weighted, or should specific sections carry more weight?

3.  Departmental Supervisor Assessment  (30%)
3.1  Site Visitation Assessment
The Departmental Supervisor conducts one site visit during the attachment period. During or after the visit, the supervisor:

•	Records qualitative observations and comments on the student's progress and the training environment.
•	Assigns a Visitation Score — a numeric percentage out of 100 — based on what was observed.

Output	One score out of 100
Submitted to	DLO for inclusion in final grade compilation
Default weight	30% of the student's final grade (customisable per department)

⚠  Decision needed (D-4): Should the system provide a structured scoring rubric to guide the Departmental Supervisor's visitation score (e.g. a checklist of observable behaviours), or should it remain a free numeric entry with open-ended comments only?

4.  DLO — Final Grade Compilation
4.1  Inputs to the Final Grade
The DLO receives the following scores and applies the department's configured weightings. The Report & Presentation component is flexible — departments choose which sub-components apply:

#	Component	Score Source	Out of	Default Weight
1	Industrial Supervisor	Final Assessment Form (20 criteria, rated 1–5, submitted once at end of term)	100	40%
2	Departmental Supervisor	Site Visitation Score	100	30%
3a	Report & Presentation	Attachment Report Score (graded by DLO)	100	30% combined
(Report only, Presentation only, or split between both)
3b		Presentation Score (graded by DLO / Panel)	100	
TOTAL	100%

The 30% Report & Presentation weight is always fixed. What changes per department is how that 30% is distributed — see Section 5 for configuration details.

4.2  Attachment Report Scoring (DLO)
The DLO is responsible for evaluating and scoring the student's final written attachment report. The report score is entered directly by the DLO as a percentage out of 100.

⚠  Decision needed (D-5): Should the system provide the DLO with a structured rubric for scoring the attachment report (e.g. criteria such as structure, content, grammar, reflection), or should it be a single free numeric entry?

4.3  Presentation Scoring (DLO / Panel)
At the end of the attachment period the student delivers a presentation assessed by the DLO and/or a panel. The presentation score is entered as a percentage out of 100.

⚠  Decision needed (D-6): Should the system provide a structured rubric for the presentation assessment (e.g. delivery, content, Q&A handling), or should it be a single free numeric entry?

5.  Per-Department Custom Grading System
5.1  Overview
Each department's grading configuration is managed by two roles with different levels of access:

Role	Access Level	What they can do
DLO	Department level	Configures and updates the grading structure and component weights for their own department only.
CLO	Super admin — all departments	Can select any department and configure or update that department's grading structure and weights on their behalf. Full override access across all departments.
HOD	No access	The HOD has no role in grading configuration and cannot set or modify any grading settings.

5.2  Grading Structure Selection
When configuring a department's grading, the DLO (or the CLO acting on a department's behalf) selects one of four grading structures using checkboxes. The selected structure determines which score input fields are activated for students in that department. Only one structure can be active at a time.

Structures A, B, and C have fixed component sets. Structure D is fully custom — all four components are always present and the DLO sets each weight freely, including setting Report or Presentation to 0% to effectively exclude them.

Structure A — Industrial Supervisor + Departmental Supervisor + Report
#	Component	Weight (set by DLO — must sum to 100%)
W1	Industrial Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 40%)
W2	Departmental Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 30%)
W3	Attachment Report Score	____%  (e.g. 30%)
Total (W1 + W2 + W3) = 100%	No Presentation is required or recorded.

Structure B — Industrial Supervisor + Departmental Supervisor + Presentation
#	Component	Weight (set by DLO — must sum to 100%)
W1	Industrial Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 40%)
W2	Departmental Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 30%)
W3	Presentation Score	____%  (e.g. 30%)
Total (W1 + W2 + W3) = 100%	No Report is required or recorded.

Structure C — Industrial Supervisor + Departmental Supervisor + Report + Presentation
#	Component	Weight (set by DLO — must sum to 100%)
W1	Industrial Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 40%)
W2	Departmental Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 30%)
W3	Attachment Report Score	____%  (e.g. 15%)
W4	Presentation Score	____%  (e.g. 15%)
Total (W1 + W2 + W3 + W4) = 100%	Both Report and Presentation are required.

Structure D — Fully Custom  (All 4 Components, Freely Weighted)
#	Component	Weight (set by DLO — must sum to 100%)
W1	Industrial Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 40%  —  minimum 1%)
W2	Departmental Supervisor Score	____%  (e.g. 30%  —  minimum 1%)
W3	Attachment Report Score	____%  (can be 0% to exclude Report entirely)
W4	Presentation Score	____%  (can be 0% to exclude Presentation entirely)
Total (W1 + W2 + W3 + W4) = 100%	W1 and W2 must each be at least 1%. W3 and W4 may be 0% (excluded). W3 + W4 together must be ≥ 0% but W1 + W2 ≤ 100%.

5.3  Who Sets the Configuration
Who can set it	DLO (for their own department)  |  CLO (for any department — super admin)
Who cannot set it	HOD — the Head of Department has no access to grading configuration.
CLO — department selection	When acting as super admin, the CLO first selects the target department from a department list, then accesses and edits that department's grading configuration exactly as the DLO would.
When is it set	At any time before students in that department are assessed.
Can it be changed	Yes — the DLO (for their own department) or the CLO (for any department) can update the configuration via a deliberate update action. Changes apply to future assessments; completed assessments retain the weights that were active at the time.

5.4  System Validation Rules
The following rules are enforced by the system when a department saves its grading configuration:

1.	Exactly one grading structure (A, B, C, or D) must be selected via checkbox before saving.
2.	Structure A: W1 + W2 + W3 (Report) must equal 100%. W1 and W2 must each be ≥ 1%.
3.	Structure B: W1 + W2 + W3 (Presentation) must equal 100%. W1 and W2 must each be ≥ 1%.
4.	Structure C: W1 + W2 + W3 (Report) + W4 (Presentation) must equal 100%. All four must be ≥ 1%.
5.	Structure D: W1 + W2 + W3 + W4 must equal 100%. W1 and W2 must each be ≥ 1%. W3 (Report) and W4 (Presentation) may be 0%.
6.	Only the DLO (for their own department) or the CLO (super admin, any department) may create or update the configuration. The HOD has no configuration access.
7.	Changing the structure later clears all previously entered weights and requires re-entry.

5.5  Final Grade Output
The final output for every student is a single percentage score out of 100. The formula varies based on the department's Report & Presentation mode:

Structure	Final Score Formula
A — Report Only	(Ind. Sup. Score × W1%)  +  (Dept. Sup. Score × W2%)  +  (Report Score × W3%)
B — Presentation Only	(Ind. Sup. Score × W1%)  +  (Dept. Sup. Score × W2%)  +  (Presentation Score × W3%)
C — Both	(Ind. Sup. Score × W1%)  +  (Dept. Sup. Score × W2%)  +  (Report Score × W3%)  +  (Presentation Score × W4%)
D — Fully Custom	(Ind. Sup. Score × W1%)  +  (Dept. Sup. Score × W2%)  +  (Report Score × W3%)  +  (Presentation Score × W4%)  where W3 and/or W4 may be 0%

6.  Full Score Flow Summary
The diagram below describes the complete journey from raw assessment input to final grade.

INDUSTRIAL SUPERVISOR
Weekly Rubric (every week — auto-calculated from term dates):
  6 criteria  |  Qualitative ratings  |  NOT SCORED — collected as progress record only

Final Assessment Form (once, end of term):
  20 criteria across 4 sections, each rated 1–5
  (Sum of ratings ÷ 100) × 100  →  Industrial Supervisor Score / 100  →  passed to DLO

DEPARTMENTAL SUPERVISOR
One site visit  →  qualitative observations + comments
Supervisor assigns Visitation Score / 100  →  passed to DLO

DLO — REPORT & PRESENTATION  (structure-dependent)
Structure A — Report Only:  Attachment Report graded by DLO  →  Score / 100
Structure B — Presentation Only:  Student Presentation graded by DLO/Panel  →  Score / 100
Structure C — Both:  Report + Presentation graded  →  Two scores / 100
Structure D — Fully Custom:  Report (W3%) and/or Presentation (W4%) — either may be 0%

Only the fields for the department's selected structure are activated in the system.

DLO — FINAL COMPILATION
Receives all active component scores and applies department's configured weights
(Structure A/B: W1+W2+W3=100%  |  Structure C/D: W1+W2+W3+W4=100%)

→  Final Percentage Score out of 100  →  Pushed to academic records
